Dont get these confused with my process

I fundamentally believe that every design leader should have set principles that they stand by throughout the course of their careers.

#1

UX is the sum of all things.

Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.

#2

Perfection doesn’t do a 9-­5.

And neither should we. Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment, feedback, and support they need. Trust everyone to get their work done. Strive for perfection, do it for your clients and end-users. Take pride and ownership of your decisions and your designs.

#3

Creator. Partner. Collaborator.

Ideas are far from linear. As Product Designer, I spearhead and facilitate a design team[s] process. These processes promote sustainable and scaleable development. With accountability from everyone, we should be able to maintain a constant pace and put out amazing design work indefinitely.

#4

Attention to Detial.

Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances not only a teams agility, but increases the likelihood of scalability over the long haul.

#5

Maintaining Inspiration.

Pick up a book, go for a walk, talk to an old friend, pet a dog, go skydiving, just stay inspired. It sounds easy, but sometimes we get so caught up in our work that we forget to take care of ourselves. Its important that we keep improving ourselves and our work as designers. What have you done for yourslef lately to keep your mind sharp?
